**Course:** GRC102 - Information Security Governance  
**Week:** 3  
**Duration:** 5 Days (Self-Paced)  
**Level:** Intermediate

## The Chaos at TechGlobal: A Governance Crisis

Welcome to **TechGlobal**, a fast-growing tech powerhouse with 2,500 employees across 5 global offices. On the surface, everything looks great—revenue is up, and the product is scaling. But beneath the hood, the security engine is sputtering.

Currently, TechGlobal’s "security strategy" is just the IT Director making every single decision on the fly. There are no formal committees, business units are doing their own thing, and the CEO and Board are essentially flying blind when it comes to cyber risk. 

As the newly appointed **Lead Security Governance Consultant**, your mission is to transform this "informal approach" into a world-class governance machine. You aren't just fixing IT; you are defining who holds the keys to the kingdom and how the ship is steered.

### Your Key Stakeholders

To succeed, you must navigate the needs of these high-stakes players:

| Name | Role | Key Concerns |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Board** | Oversight | Regulatory fines, brand reputation, and "Are we actually safe?" |
| **The CEO** | Executive | Business growth vs. security friction, strategic alignment. |
| **IT Director** | Current "Owner" | Overwhelmed by decisions, needs a structured hand-off. |
| **BU Leaders** | Operations | "Don't let security slow down my department's output." |

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this lab, you will be able to:

- **Analyze** and dismantle ineffective security structures.
- **Architect** a modern security governance framework.
- **Define** high-impact roles like the CISO, BISO, and Security Architect.
- **Design** cross-functional committees that actually get things done.
- **Implement** accountability using the industry-standard RACI matrix.

## Task 1: Architecting the Governance Structure (Day 1)

**Scenario:** TechGlobal is currently a "Security Wild West." The IT Director is the sheriff, judge, and executioner, which is a recipe for burnout and massive security gaps. The Board has given you a blank canvas to design a formal structure.

**Your Mission:** Design a governance hierarchy that bridges the gap between the server room and the boardroom.

### Activity: Structural Design
1. **Analyze the Gaps:** Identify the top 3 risks of TechGlobal's current "informal" approach.
2. **The Blueprint:** Create an organizational chart that includes:
   - Board-level oversight.
   - Executive leadership (C-Suite).
   - Specialized security management and operational roles.
   - Integration points for Business Units.

### Deliverables for Task 1
- **Security Governance Org Chart:** A clear diagram showing reporting lines and communication paths.
- **The Justification Report (1-2 Pages):** Explain *why* this structure fits a 2,500-employee tech firm and how it solves the "visibility" problem for the CEO.

---

## Task 2: Defining the Guardians (Day 2)

**Scenario:** A structure is just a box on a page without the right people. You need to define exactly what the new "Guardians of TechGlobal" will do.

**Your Mission:** Develop "Battle Cards" (Role Definitions) for five critical positions to ensure no one is stepping on each other's toes.

### Activity: Role Profiling
Develop comprehensive profiles for:
1. **CISO (Chief Information Security Officer)**
2. **Security Operations Manager**
3. **BISO (Business Information Security Officer)**
4. **Security Governance Specialist**
5. **Security Architect**

### Deliverables for Task 2
- **The Role Definition Dossier:** For each role, you must provide:
  - **Purpose:** Why does this role exist?
  - **The "Top 8":** Eight core responsibilities.
  - **KPIs:** Three metrics that prove they are succeeding.
  - **Authority:** Who do they report to, and what can they sign off on?

---

## Task 3: The War Room - Committee Design (Day 3-4)

**Scenario:** Security is a team sport. You need to create the "War Rooms" where different departments meet to align on risk and strategy.

**Your Mission:** Design a committee ecosystem that ensures security isn't just an "IT problem."

### Activity: Committee Charters
Design the following:
- **Executive Security Council:** The heavy hitters (CEO, CFO, CISO).
- **Security Steering Committee:** The tactical engine.
- **Specialized Working Groups:** (e.g., Incident Response or Cloud Security).
- **Business Unit Security Forum:** Where the rubber meets the road.

### Deliverables for Task 3
- **Committee Charters:** A document detailing the purpose, membership, and "Decision Power" of each group.
- **Interaction Diagram:** How does information flow from a Working Group up to the Board?
- **The Governance Calendar:** A sample 12-month schedule of meetings and key milestones.

---

## Task 4: The Accountability Map - RACI (Day 5)

**Scenario:** When everyone is responsible, no one is responsible. You need to map out exactly who is **Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed** for TechGlobal's key activities.

**Your Mission:** Create the ultimate accountability matrix to prevent "finger-pointing" during a crisis.

### Activity: Mapping the RACI
Map at least 15 activities (e.g., Policy Approval, Incident Management, Budget Allocation) against roles like the Board, CISO, CIO, and End Users.

### Deliverables for Task 4
- **The TechGlobal RACI Matrix:** A detailed table/spreadsheet.
- **Implementation Guide (1 Page):** How should the CISO use this matrix to resolve conflicts?

## Submission & Grading

### Submission Guidelines
Compile all parts into a single, professional PDF. Ensure your diagrams are high-resolution and your justifications are grounded in industry frameworks like **NIST 800-39** or **ISO 27014**.

### Grading Rubric (Summary)
- **Governance Structure (30%):** Is it scalable and logical?
- **Role Definitions (25%):** Are the responsibilities clear and conflict-free?
- **Committee Design (25%):** Does it enable cross-functional collaboration?
- **RACI Matrix (20%):** Is accountability clearly defined for critical tasks?
