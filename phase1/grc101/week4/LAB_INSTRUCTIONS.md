## Task 1: NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) Deep Dive (Day 1)

### Objective

To research the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 and apply its principles to a fictional company.

### Scenario: SecureHealth

> `SecureHealth` is a regional healthcare provider with three clinics. They have a small IT team and have recently experienced a minor data breach. They want to adopt a cybersecurity framework to mature their security program and protect patient data more effectively. They have a limited budget and need a flexible, risk-based approach.

### Step-by-Step Instructions

1.  **Step 1: Research NIST CSF 2.0**
    *   Visit the official NIST Cybersecurity Framework website: [https://www.nist.gov/cyberframework](https://www.nist.gov/cyberframework)
    *   Download and review the primary NIST CSF 2.0 document: [https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/CSWP/NIST.CSWP.29.pdf](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/CSWP/NIST.CSWP.29.pdf)

2.  **Step 2: Write a Research Summary**
    *   In your own words, write a summary of the NIST CSF 2.0.
    *   Explain the purpose of the framework and its importance in the cybersecurity landscape.
    *   Describe each of the six core functions and their roles.

3.  **Step 3: Apply to SecureHealth**
    *   Read the `SecureHealth` case study carefully.
    *   Create a high-level plan for how `SecureHealth` could use the NIST CSF 2.0 to improve its cybersecurity posture.
    *   Your plan should address all six functions and be practical for a small organization with a limited budget.

A Markdown document (`task1_report.md`) containing your research summary and the high-level plan for `SecureHealth`.

## Tips for Effective Research

1.  **Start Broad, Then Narrow:** Begin with general overviews of each framework, then dive into specific controls or requirements.
2.  **Use Multiple Sources:** Don't rely on a single source. Cross-reference information from official sources and reputable third-party resources.
3.  **Take Detailed Notes:** As you research, take notes on key concepts, definitions, and examples. This will help you write your reports more effectively.
4.  **Understand the "Why":** Don't just memorize controls or requirements. Understand the reasoning behind them and how they address real-world security challenges.
5.  **Look for Practical Examples:** Seek out real-world examples and case studies that illustrate how frameworks are applied in practice.
6.  **Ask Questions:** If something doesn't make sense, research it further or ask your instructor for clarification.

## Troubleshooting Research Issues

-   **Resource Not Available:** If a link is broken or a resource is not available, try searching for an alternative source or contact your instructor.
-   **Paywall or Registration Required:** Many official resources are free, but some may require registration or purchase. Free alternatives are provided in this guide.
-   **Information Overload:** If you feel overwhelmed by the amount of information, focus on the key concepts listed for each task and build from there.
-   **Conflicting Information:** If you find conflicting information from different sources, prioritize official sources and document the discrepancy in your report.
