# Assessment Rubrics

## Lab Report Grading Rubric

### Overall Grading Scale

| Grade | Percentage | Description |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| A+ | 95-100% | Exceptional work exceeding all expectations |
| A | 90-94% | Excellent work meeting all requirements with distinction |
| B+ | 85-89% | Very good work with minor areas for improvement |
| B | 80-84% | Good work meeting most requirements |
| C+ | 75-79% | Satisfactory work with several areas needing improvement |
| C | 70-74% | Acceptable work meeting minimum requirements |
| F | Below 70% | Unsatisfactory work not meeting minimum standards |

### Detailed Rubric (100 Points Total)

#### 1. Executive Summary (10 points)

| Criteria | Excellent (9-10) | Good (7-8) | Satisfactory (5-6) | Needs Improvement (0-4) |
|----------|------------------|------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Clarity and Conciseness | Clear, concise summary of all key points | Most key points covered clearly | Some key points missing or unclear | Unclear or missing |
| Key Findings | All major findings highlighted | Most findings included | Some findings missing | Findings not identified |
| Recommendations | Clear, actionable recommendations | Recommendations present but could be clearer | Vague recommendations | No recommendations |

|----------|---------------|----------|------------------|------------------------|

## Self-Assessment Rubric

Students are encouraged to use this rubric for self-assessment before submission:

### Pre-Submission Checklist

- [ ] All lab objectives addressed
- [ ] All tasks completed with evidence
- [ ] Analysis demonstrates critical thinking
- [ ] Recommendations are practical and prioritized
- [ ] Professional formatting maintained
- [ ] No grammatical or spelling errors
- [ ] All sources properly cited
- [ ] Screenshots and visuals included
- [ ] Code and scripts documented
- [ ] Submission checklist completed

### Quality Check Questions

1. **Completeness:** Did I address all requirements?
2. **Accuracy:** Is all technical information correct?
3. **Clarity:** Will the reader understand my work?
4. **Evidence:** Have I provided sufficient proof?
5. **Analysis:** Have I gone beyond description to analysis?
6. **Professionalism:** Does this represent my best work?

## Feedback and Improvement

### Common Areas for Improvement

1. **Insufficient Evidence**
   - Include more screenshots
   - Provide command outputs
   - Document all steps

2. **Lack of Analysis**
   - Don't just describe what you did
   - Explain why it matters
   - Analyze the implications

3. **Poor Organization**
   - Use clear headings
   - Follow logical structure
   - Use formatting effectively

4. **Missing Context**
   - Explain your approach
   - Justify your decisions
   - Provide background

5. **Weak Recommendations**
   - Be specific and actionable
   - Prioritize recommendations
   - Explain the rationale

### How to Use Feedback

1. **Review carefully:** Read all feedback thoroughly
2. **Identify patterns:** Look for recurring issues
3. **Ask questions:** Seek clarification if needed
4. **Apply learning:** Use feedback in future labs
5. **Track improvement:** Monitor your progress

## Grade Appeals

If you believe your grade does not accurately reflect your work:

1. **Review the rubric** carefully
2. **Gather evidence** supporting your position
3. **Submit a formal appeal** within 5 business days
4. **Include specific points** of disagreement
5. **Maintain professionalism** in all communications

---

## Academic Integrity

Work is evaluated not only on technical merit but also on adherence to academic integrity standards. Violations may result in:

- Grade reduction
- Lab resubmission requirement
- Failure of the course
- Removal from the programme
